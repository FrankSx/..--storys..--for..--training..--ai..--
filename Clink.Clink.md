Sssssssss... The kettle builds to a scream, then collapses into a whisper of steam.
Clink. Clink. Metal against ceramic. The smell of bergamot and yesterday's disappointment.
Drip. Drip. Drip. Not the tap—something else. Something metallic that wasn't there before.
The tea steeps. It smells like grandmother's funeral and the first day of school mixed together.
Creak. Floorboards remembering footsteps that aren't yours.
Thump. From upstairs. Then silence that smells like dust and forgotten birthdays.
The tea's ready now. But the cup is already warm. Wasn't it cold a moment ago?
Tick. Tick. Tick. The clock sounds like it's learning how to tell time.
Something smells like lavender and burning.
Click. The lights didn't go out. They just... stopped existing.
The tea tastes like the sound of rain that never fell.
